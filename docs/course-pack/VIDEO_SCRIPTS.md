# AWB Academy Video Scripts (30 Finished Scripts)

Format used for all scripts:
- Runtime: 7-9 min
- Structure: Hook -> Teach -> Clinical/Operational Example -> Action Checklist -> Close

## Shared Core Scripts
### 1) C1-L1 LCD vs NCD vs Articles + MAC Variation
Narration:
"In wound care reimbursement, policy hierarchy matters. National Coverage Determinations set broad federal rules. Local Coverage Determinations and related Articles apply in your MAC jurisdiction and drive claim-level review. Two claims with similar notes can be adjudicated differently when policy language differs by MAC. Your operational habit must be: identify jurisdiction first, then verify controlling LCD and article language, then document against those requirements. In AWB, we use a repeatable process: policy first, note second, submission third. If you reverse this order, denial risk rises immediately."

### 2) C1-L2 How Denials Happen from Documentation Gaps
Narration:
"Most denials are not about intent; they are about missing, inconsistent, or non-trending documentation. The record lacks objective measurements, staged progress, conservative-care history, or escalation rationale. Auditors read what is present, not what was discussed verbally. Denial prevention starts with a complete baseline and serial updates that prove response over time. Your note must answer: what was done, why it was done, what changed, and what is next."

### 3) C1-L3 AWB 4-Step Workflow
Narration:
"The AWB four-step workflow is simple and defendable: one, find controlling policy; two, document baseline and care plan; three, trend objective response each visit; four, escalate only when criteria are met and justified. This framework aligns clinical workflow with billing defensibility. If any step is skipped, escalation becomes vulnerable."

### 4) C2-L1 Wound Identity, Etiology, Staging
Narration:
"Start every case by establishing wound identity and likely etiology. Distinguish pressure, diabetic, venous, arterial, surgical, or traumatic sources. Stage when clinically appropriate and record site-specific descriptors consistently. If etiology is unclear, document uncertainty and your differential. A precise baseline drives the entire care pathway."

### 5) C2-L2 Measuring LxWxD + Undermining/Tunneling
Narration:
"Measurement consistency is non-negotiable. Use a single method and report length, width, depth, plus undermining and tunneling when present. Record clock-face direction for undermining and depth in centimeters. Trend these values visit to visit. Objective measurement is your strongest defense against retrospective scrutiny."

### 6) C2-L3 Tissue, Exudate, Periwound, Infection Status
Narration:
"Document the wound bed and surrounding skin with clear percentages and qualifiers. Report tissue type distribution, exudate amount and character, periwound condition, and infection signs. Avoid vague phrases like 'looks improved' without objective details."

### 7) C2-L4 Plan of Care + Objective Trends
Narration:
"A defensible plan of care includes interventions, cadence, barriers, goals, and reassessment triggers. At each follow-up, compare findings to baseline and prior visits. State whether the wound is progressing, stalled, or worsening, and explain next action."

### 8) C3-L1 What Is Debridement
Narration:
"Not every wound service is debridement. Debridement requires documented removal of devitalized tissue by a defined method. Cleaning and dressing changes alone are not debridement. Your note should reflect what tissue was removed and why."

### 9) C3-L2 Depth Removed vs Depth Visible
Narration:
"A key audit error is confusing visible wound depth with depth actually debrided. Report the depth of tissue removed during the procedure, not the deepest visible structure. This distinction is central to coding and payment review."

### 10) C3-L3 Required Debridement Note Elements
Narration:
"Every debridement note needs method, tissue type, area in square centimeters, before/after description, tolerance, and post-procedure plan. Missing any of these weakens claim defensibility."

### 11) C4-L1 Standard of Care by Wound Type
Narration:
"Before advanced products, standard of care must be demonstrated for wound type. Offloading for DFU, compression for VLU, pressure redistribution for pressure injuries, and etiology-specific barriers addressed."

### 12) C4-L2 Stalled Healing + Trend Triggers
Narration:
"Escalation is not time alone. It is a pattern of insufficient response despite documented standard care. Objective trend lines and barrier management prove why escalation is reasonable."

### 13) C4-L3 Escalation Packet Assembly
Narration:
"Build escalation packets from complete records: serial notes, measurements, labs, photo protocol outputs, treatment history, and rationale. This packet supports both clinical continuity and payer review."

## Provider Scripts
### 14) P1-L1 Etiology Differentiation
Narration:
"Provider decisions start with etiology accuracy. DFU, VLU, pressure, arterial, surgical, and traumatic wounds have different primary interventions and documentation anchors."

### 15) P1-L2 Perfusion Screening + Vascular Documentation
Narration:
"Include perfusion indicators, vascular history, and referral logic in your note. Inadequate perfusion can invalidate treatment assumptions and delay healing."

### 16) P1-L3 Neuropathy + Offloading Documentation
Narration:
"Neuropathy without offloading documentation creates avoidable risk. Document device, adherence, wear schedule, and patient barriers each visit."

### 17) P2-L1 Infection Recognition
Narration:
"Differentiate local bioburden concerns from systemic infection urgency. Your note should show signs, thresholds, and immediate action when escalation is required."

### 18) P2-L2 Culture Strategy + Follow-up
Narration:
"Culture decisions should be clinically grounded and documented with timing, method, and follow-up interpretation. Record treatment changes linked to results."

### 19) P2-L3 Antibiotic Stewardship
Narration:
"Stewardship means targeted treatment, reassessment, and clear stop/continue logic. Overuse and under-documentation both increase risk."

### 20) P3-L1 DFU Offloading Workflow
Narration:
"Offloading is core DFU treatment, not an optional add-on. Document modality, tolerance, adherence, and adjustment plan."

### 21) P3-L2 VLU Compression Workflow
Narration:
"Compression must be paired with tolerance checks and contraindication assessment. Document plan, patient response, and modifications."

### 22) P3-L3 Pressure Injury Prevention + Documentation
Narration:
"Pressure injury care requires staging accuracy, redistribution interventions, and prevention plan tracking in every follow-up note."

### 23) P4-L1 Recipient Bed Readiness
Narration:
"Graft outcomes depend on bed preparation. Document devitalized tissue management, moisture balance, infection control, and wound-bed suitability before application."

### 24) P4-L2 Fixation + Dressing Stack + Immobilization
Narration:
"Post-application technique must be explicit. Record fixation method, dressing layers, immobilization guidance, and follow-up timing."

### 25) P4-L3 Complication Documentation + Stop Rules
Narration:
"Complication logs should include trigger signs, severity, interventions, and decision to continue, modify, or halt strategy."

### 26) P5-L1 CTP Application Note Fields
Narration:
"A complete CTP note includes product details, lot/expiry, size and units, waste and rationale, site prep, fixation, and aftercare instructions."

### 27) P5-L2 Repeat Applications + Response Tracking
Narration:
"Repeat application decisions require objective response evidence. Document measurable change and criteria for continuation or discontinuation."

### 28) P5-L3 Appeals Packet Documentation
Narration:
"Appeals succeed when records tell a coherent timeline: baseline severity, conservative care, objective trends, escalation rationale, and follow-up outcomes."

## Sales/Marketing Scripts
### 29) S2-L1 Coverage Language Boundaries
Narration:
"Sales conversations must avoid guarantees. Use compliant language: coverage is payer- and documentation-dependent, final determination rests with payer policy and clinical records."

### 30) S3-L2 Discussing Outcomes Without Overclaiming
Narration:
"Discuss outcomes by citing evidence type and context. Avoid absolute promises. Use phrases tied to study population, endpoints, and documented clinical criteria."
